My trip was from 6th June 2026 to 16th June 2026. The USA part of the trip was mostly around San Francisco and the Bay Area.

The trip was an office trip (June 8 and 9), with me keeping a few days separately for personal travel.

So my overall trip ended up being these flights:
- 6th June: Delhi to Tokyo
- 6th/7th June: Tokyo to San Francisco
- 15th June: San Francisco to Tokyo
- 16th June: Tokyo to India

Oh btw, I had carried 300 USD in cash.

### Day 6 (11th June, 2026)

Woke up, had oats for breakfast at home, and then went to the [Computer History Museum](https://maps.app.goo.gl/x9CGtvm2qxgL9HjS7). That was GREAT.

They had a screening room where Apple products were displayed. The guy at the reception told me that they keep changing things up in the room. Before Apple stuff, IIRC it was video games / retro games and stuff like that. After Apple, it would be some Google networking stuff. He told me that they had to reach out to some senior Google person who then had to fly here and there in the country to find stuff they could display. The reception guy also said that what they display is like 2% of the stored collection, which is insane.

The exhibition itself had a lot of sections. I came out to the cafe to eat something, then joined the tour at 12 PM.

The 12 PM tour was awesome, it went for 1.5 hours. A 60+ years old guy, Jim Coleman/Coltman maybe, gave us the tour. He worked at Bell Labs for 23 years and then at other places.

Mind-blowing moments:
1. Gordon Moore, the guy behind Moore's Law, is one of the founders of Intel.
2. Xerox PARC invented so much stuff: laser printers, Ethernet, GUI, etc.
3. Before 1945, if you looked in the dictionary, "computer" meant "a person who computes". Insane.

Computer History Museum was awesome. I punched "Akshat Jain" on a couple of punch cards and brought them back with me. The shop didn't have good t-shirts. Bought a pen and a fridge magnet. Saw Apple I and Apple II and Lisa and Macintosh in the exhibition area, yay!

I spent like 6 hours at CHM. It was insanely fun.

Then went to [Peet's Coffee](https://maps.app.goo.gl/9qTyznjaCsUgo2n1A), and IIRC had a croissant and some cold coffee which was good!

From there, I booked an Uber to [Apple Park Visitor Center](https://maps.app.goo.gl/WwFKrXWhv71vgNiq8). But I realised I could also go to Steve Jobs' garage on the route, so I added an intermediary stop for that.

So we stopped at [Steve Jobs' garage](https://maps.app.goo.gl/JL1vFh6rLsyHR8GF7), the driver guy clicked a few photos of me, then we came to Apple Park Visitor Center.
